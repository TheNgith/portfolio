Hi, I’m Thinh Nguyen, an undergraduate student in Computer Engineering with a minor in Mathematics at the University of South Florida. My interests lie at the intersection of machine learning, high-performance computing, and hardware acceleration.

I’ve worked as a Machine Learning Engineer Intern at USF’s High-Performance Computing Lab, where I helped develop an accelerated genomic sequence alignment system.

I enjoy designing efficient computational pipelines - whether it’s optimizing data processing in Python and C/CUDA or exploring low-level system design through Verilog and FPGA projects. I’m driven by curiosity for how software and hardware can collaborate to push the boundaries of performance and intelligence.
